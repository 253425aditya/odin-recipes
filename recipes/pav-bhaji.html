Pav-Bhaji

Pav Bhaji is a popular Indian street food that originated in Mumbai.
It consists of "Pav" (soft bread rolls) served with "Bhaji" (a spicy, flavorful vegetable mash).
This dish is a perfect blend of textures and spices, making it a favorite comfort food for all age groups.



indian-vegan-pav-bhaji-recipe-Ingredients

  • 2 cups boiled and mashed potatoes
  • 1 cup cauliflower (chopped)
  • 1 cup green peas
  • 1 cup carrots (chopped)
  • 1 medium capsicum (chopped)
  • 3 large tomatoes (chopped or pureed)
  • 2 medium onions (chopped)
  • 2-3 tbsp pav bhaji masala
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1-2 tsp red chili powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 2-3 tbsp butter
  • 1 tbsp cooking oil
  • 1/2 lemon (juice)
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish
  • 6-8 pav buns
  • Butter for toasting


Steps to Make Pav Bhaji
  1. Boil potatoes, cauliflower, carrots, and peas. Lightly mash them.
  2. Heat butter and oil in a pan, sauté onions until golden, and add ginger-garlic paste.
  3. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until soft.
  4. Mix in pav bhaji masala, red chili powder, and salt. Sauté briefly.
  5. Add the boiled vegetables, mash them, and adjust consistency with water.
  6. Simmer for 10–15 minutes, then add lemon juice and garnish with coriander.
  7. Slice pav buns and toast them with butter on a hot pan.

Serve hot with a dollop of butter, onions, and lemon wedges!
