Chole Bhature

Chole Bhature is a classic North Indian dish that combines spicy, flavorful chickpeas (**Chole**) with soft, fluffy deep-fried bread (**Bhature**).
It is a popular breakfast or lunch option, loved for its rich taste and satisfying combination.



Ingredients for Chole Bhature

  • 1 cup chickpeas (soaked overnight)
  • 2 medium onions (finely chopped)
  • 3 large tomatoes (pureed)
  • 2 tbsp oil
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 green chilies (optional)
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1 tsp dry mango powder (amchur)
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour (for Bhature)
  • 1/4 cup yogurt
  • 1/2 tsp baking soda
  • Oil for deep frying


Steps to Make Chole Bhature
  1. Prepare Chole:
    1. Pressure cook soaked chickpeas with water, salt, and a pinch of turmeric until soft.
    2. Heat oil in a pan, add cumin seeds, and sauté onions until golden brown.
    3. Add ginger-garlic paste, green chilies, and tomato puree. Cook until the oil separates.
    4. Mix in spices (coriander, garam masala, turmeric, red chili powder, dry mango powder).
    5. Add boiled chickpeas and some of the cooking water. Simmer for 10–15 minutes to thicken the gravy.
    6. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves.
  2. Prepare Bhature:
    1. In a bowl, mix all-purpose flour, yogurt, baking soda, and a pinch of salt. Knead into a soft dough using water.
    2. Cover and let it rest for 1–2 hours.
    3. Divide the dough into small portions, roll them into circles or ovals.
    4. Heat oil in a deep pan and fry each bhature until puffed and golden brown.

Serve the hot Chole with freshly fried Bhature, along with onion rings and a lemon wedge!
