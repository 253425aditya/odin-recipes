Samosa

Samosa is a popular Indian snack that is crispy, flaky, and filled with a delicious spicy potato mixture.
It is a favorite street food enjoyed with tamarind or mint chutney and perfect for tea-time.



Ingredients for Samosa

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup oil or ghee
  • 1/2 tsp carom seeds (ajwain)
  • Salt to taste
  • 1/2 cup water (for dough)
  • 4 medium potatoes (boiled and mashed)
  • 1/2 cup green peas
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 1/2 tsp garam masala
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1/2 tsp red chili powder
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 2-3 tbsp oil (for filling)
  • Oil for deep frying


Steps to Make Samosa
  1. Prepare Dough: Mix flour, salt, carom seeds, and oil. Add water gradually to form a stiff dough. Cover and let rest for 30 minutes.
  2. Prepare Filling: Heat oil in a pan, add cumin seeds, ginger-garlic paste, and spices (coriander, garam masala, turmeric, chili powder). Add mashed potatoes and peas. Mix well and cook for 5 minutes.
  3. Let the filling cool completely.
  4. Shape Samosas: Roll the dough into small circles. Cut each circle in half to form semi-circles. Fold each semi-circle into a cone shape, fill with the potato mixture, and seal the edges with water.
  5. Fry Samosas: Heat oil in a deep pan. Fry samosas on medium heat until golden and crisp.

Serve hot with tamarind or mint chutney!
